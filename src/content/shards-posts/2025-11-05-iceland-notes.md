Five small things from a week spent driving the south coast.

**1.** The light. In late autumn the sun barely lifts off the horizon. Every
hour is the kind of golden hour landscape photographers chase. After two
days you stop noticing it; on the way home you miss it for weeks.

**2.** Wind. Not a metaphor. Doors will be torn out of your hands. Plan for it.

**3.** Black sand. The grain is heavier than it looks; the wind drives it
hard enough to strip paint. Keep your back to it.

**4.** Quiet. There are stretches of road where you can park, switch off the
engine, and hear absolutely nothing — no traffic, no people, no animals.
This is rarer than I had assumed.

**5.** The map lies. Distances on a topographic map are short. Distances on
Icelandic roads, in November, with two passengers and weather coming in,
are not. Allow twice what the map suggests.
