After two seasons with a stock mirrorless, I picked up an ASI533MC Pro. Same
scope, same mount — different sensor entirely. The first night out was a
useful re-grounding in what cooling and 16-bit data actually buy you.

## What changed immediately

- **Dark current** disappeared. The dedicated cooler holds the sensor 30 °C
  below ambient; what used to be a thermal hot mess at the bottom of every
  frame is now flat.
- **Stretching room** is enormous. 16-bit raw with low read noise lets me
  push a stretch much further before the histogram falls apart.
- **Workflow** is simpler. No menu surfing for ISO and white balance — gain
  set once, offset set once, and SharpCap drives the rest.

## What didn't change

The sky. Bortle 5 is still Bortle 5, and the limiting factor is light
pollution, not the camera. The new sensor finds faint structure faster, but
the same dim targets still need long integration. There is no shortcut.

## Notes to self

- Calibrate flats with the new sensor — old flats are useless.
- Pixel scale is finer; revisit guiding cadence.
- The amp glow that everyone warned about is real but predictable; dark
  frames remove it cleanly.
