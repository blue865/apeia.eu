You read about Bortle 2 sites. You see the maps and the SQM numbers. None of
it prepares you for the first time you look up.

The Milky Way is not a smudge. It is structure — a ribbon with edges, knots,
and dust lanes you can see with your naked eye. The fact that this is the
default sky for most of human history, and that we have lit it away in two
generations, is one of the quieter losses of our age.

## What changed

I brought a small wide-field rig: a 50mm lens, static tripod, eight-second
subs. From a Bortle 2 site, that's enough to capture detail that would take
hours of integration from home. Light pollution is by far the largest cost
in any astrophotography budget.

## What didn't

My patience. After two hours my hands were too cold to operate the camera
properly. Take warmer gloves next time.
